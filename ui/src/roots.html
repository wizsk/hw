Arabic Verb Families (Form I, II, III, etc.)

Learn how different verb forms are derived from the same root

Form I (فَعَلَ)

The basic verb form. It usually represents simple action or state.

Example: كتب (Kataba) - "He wrote"
Literal Meaning: "He wrote"

Form II (فَعَّلَ)

Form II often involves causation or intensification of the meaning of the root.

Example: كَتَّبَ (Kattaba) - "He made (someone) write"
Literal Meaning: "He caused to write"

Form III (فَاعَلَ)

Form III generally implies participation or interaction in the action.

Example: شارَكَ (Shaarka) - "He participated"
Literal Meaning: "He shared/He engaged in (an activity)"

Form IV (أَفْعَلَ)

Form IV generally implies a causative action similar to Form II but with a more direct sense of causing.

Example: أَكَّدَ (Akkada) - "He confirmed"
Literal Meaning: "He made (it) certain"

Form V (تَفَعَّلَ)

Form V is similar to Form II, but it usually implies a reflexive or reciprocal action.

Example: تَفَجَّأَ (Tafajja'a) - "He was surprised"
Literal Meaning: "He was struck with surprise"

Form VI (تَفَاعَلَ)

Form VI generally refers to a mutual or reciprocal action.

Example: تَشارَكَ (Tashaarka) - "They shared"
Literal Meaning: "They mutually participated"

Form VII (اِمْتَفَعَلَ)

Form VII typically indicates a passive or intransitive verb.

Example: اِمْتَحَنَ (Imtahana) - "He was tested"
Literal Meaning: "He underwent testing"

Form VIII (اِفْتَعَلَ)

Form VIII usually involves a meaning of self-action or reflexive action.

Example: اِشْتَرَكَ (Ishtaraka) - "He joined"
Literal Meaning: "He engaged in (something)"

Form IX (اِفْعَلَّ)

Form IX generally relates to colors or defects.

Example: اِحْمَرَّ (Ihmarra) - "He turned red"
Literal Meaning: "He became red"

Form X (اِسْتَفْعَلَ)

Form X typically involves seeking or requesting an action.

Example: اِسْتَفْهَمَ (Istafhama) - "He asked"
Literal Meaning: "He sought understanding"
